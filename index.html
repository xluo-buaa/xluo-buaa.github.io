Professor of Mathematics

I am a professor in the School of Mathematical Sciences at Beihang University. My research focuses on nonlinear filtering, estimation theory and forecasting, etc.

My current work involves developing nonlinear filtering theory and algorithms, as well as their applications in engineering, economics. I have published over 30 papers in peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings.

Research Projects

Funded research projects and collaborations

2023.1-2026.12

The study on the feedback particle filter for the nonlinear filtering problems with jump observations

National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) General Program, No. 12271019

In this project, we develop the feedback particle filter (FPF) for the nonlinear filtering (NLF) with jump observation, and analyze its convergence and error. As well, the challenges like numerical solution of control gain in the high-dimensional NLF problems are addressed.

Nonlinear filtering Jump observations Kushner-Stratonovich-Poisson equation
2019.1-2022.12

The study on the splitting-up methods of nonlinear filtering problems with correlated noises

National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) General Program, No. 11871003

In this project, we study the splitting-up methods of two simplified nonlinear filtering models based on Zakai equation and feedback particle filter (FPF). The convergence analysis is also investigated.

Nonlinear filtering Zakai equation splitting-up method correlated noises convergence analysis
2015.1-2017.12

The study on the real-time algorithm of nonlinear filtering problems

National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) Young Scientists Fund Project, No. 11501023

In this project, we study the real-time on- and off-line algorithm for solving nonlinear filtering problems with moderate high-dimensional states. In the off-line part, the suitable basis functions are chosen in the Galerkin spectral method to solve the Kolmogorov forward equation. In the on-line part, we use the Gauss quadrature rule for numerical integrations and the CPU parallel computing is helpful in dealing with high-dimensional states problems.

Nonlinear filtering real-time algorithm spectral method
2015.1-2016.6

Solving the scalar nonlinear conservation laws by Hermite spectral viscosity method and the design of filter techniques

the Beijing Natural Science Foundation, Youth Project, No. 1154011

In this project, we study a viscosity vanishing spectral method based on generalized Hermite functions, which can numerically solve nonlinear conservation law equations, and theoretically prove that this method converges to the unique entropy solution of the nonlinear conservation law. In order to eliminate the Gibbs phenomenon, we post-process the numerical solutions obtained earlier, that is, design a filtering method that can restore the high-precision of the spectral method and theoretically prove the effectiveness of the filtering.

viscosity vanishing spectral method nonlinear conservation law Gibbs phenomenon

Teaching

Courses taught at Beihang University

Probability Theory

Undergraduate Level (2rd Year)

Fall Semester

Probability theory is a foundational branch of mathematics that studies the likelihood of random events occurring, using concepts like sample spaces, event sets, and probability axioms (e.g., non-negativity, additivity) to quantify uncertainty. It introduces core tools such as probability rules (addition, multiplication), conditional probability, and basic distributions (uniform, binomial, normal), forming the basis for understanding randomness in fields from statistics to everyday decision-making.

3.5 Credits

Mathematical English

Undergraduate Level

Fall Semester

Mathematical English focuses on teaching English terminology, academic expressions, and literature reading skills in the field of mathematics, helping students communicate mathematical ideas and read international mathematical papers effectively.

1 Credit

Bayesian statistics

Undergraduate Level (3rd Year)

Spring Semester

Bayesian Statistics centers on Bayes' theorem, teaching students to quantify uncertainty by combining prior knowledge with observed data--shifting from the frequentist view of "fixed parameters" to treating parameters as random variables with probabilistic beliefs.

3 Credits
